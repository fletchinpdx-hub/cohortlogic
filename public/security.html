Security & privacy

Our tools are safe, but treat student data carefully on your end, too.

There are two kinds of data here, and we treat them differently. The student roster and schedule data you work with in our tools never leave your browser. We never see it, never store it, never transmit it. The contact information you give us when you sign up — your name, email, and school — is stored so we can manage your account. That's the full picture. While we're still maturing our security program, please anonymize your student data. Use student IDs instead of names.

Honest note We're not yet SOC 2 audited or part of formal DPA programs. We won't claim we are until we are. More on this page as our security program matures.

What we do today

The commitments we're making right now.

A short list, on purpose. We'd rather make a few promises we'll keep than a long list we can't.

01

Student data never leaves your browser

Class Builder and Schedule Builder process everything locally in your browser tab. Your roster and schedule data are never transmitted to our servers. Close the tab and they're gone.

02

Account info is stored to manage your trial

When you sign up, we store your name, email, role, and school so we can set up your access and stay in touch. That's the only data we hold about you. We don't sell it or share it with anyone.

03

We don't train on your data

We never use student rosters, schedule data, or your account information to train models, ours or anyone else's. The solver is classical optimization, not generative AI.

04

No third-party sharing

We don't pipe your information to analytics tools, ad networks, or data brokers. The student and schedule data never reaches us at all. The account info you provide stays with us.

What we ask of you

Anonymize at the source. We'll do our part.

Even though we don't store your data, less identifying information leaving your school is always better. A simple rule of thumb: nothing in the file you'd be uncomfortable seeing on a screen at a coffee shop.

A few simple guidelines

  • ·Use student ID numbers instead of first and last names. Keep the lookup table on your end.
  • ·Skip dates of birth. Grade level is enough for the solver.
  • ·Aggregate sensitive flags (IEP / ELL / 504) into a Yes / No column rather than including full notes.
  • ·If you need to capture a parent or teacher request, paraphrase it (e.g. "separate from S-00483") rather than including names.
As we grow

Our security program will grow with us.

Things like formal SOC 2 audits, signed DPAs, and regional data residency are areas we're paying attention to. We'll publish each one here when it's real, not before. If your district has specific security requirements, we want to hear about them.
