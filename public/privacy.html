Privacy Policy

What we collect, why, and how we protect it.

Cohort Logic builds tools for schools. Some of those tools handle student data. We take that seriously and will tell you exactly what we store, where it lives, and what we commit to protecting.

Last updated: June 5, 2026

Where we are today

Cohort Logic is an early-stage product. We are not yet SOC 2 audited and we have not signed formal FERPA Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with our infrastructure providers. We won't claim those things until they're real. What we can say with certainty: our infrastructure is school-scoped with row-level security, student data is never used for anything other than delivering the service, and we will tell you before anything changes.

Our products

Different tools, different data approaches.

🖥️

Class Builder

Runs entirely in your browser. Student data from your spreadsheet or Google Sheet is never uploaded to Cohort Logic's servers. When you close the tab, it's gone. This is the architecture — not a setting.

📋

Check-in / Check-out (CICO)

A behavioral tracking tool that stores data server-side so multiple staff members can access it. Student records, daily check-ins, and scores are stored in a secured database. See below for what's stored and how it's protected.

What CICO stores.

When your school uses CICO, the following data is stored in Cohort Logic's database:

Data type What's included Why
Student records First name, last name, grade level To identify students in check-in flows
Daily check-ins Date, overall score, notes Core check-in record
Period scores Score per class period (0–2 scale) Detailed behavioral tracking
Incidents Incident type and description Optional additional context per check-in
Staff accounts Name, email address, school affiliation Authentication and school-level access control

FERPA.

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) governs how student education records are handled. When your school uses CICO, your school remains the responsible party for student data under FERPA. Cohort Logic acts as a service provider operating under your school's authority.

What we've implemented to support FERPA compliance:

  • School-level data isolation — each school's data is inaccessible to users from other schools, enforced at the database level with row-level security policies.
  • FERPA audit log — all inserts, updates, and deletes to student records are logged with a timestamp and the user who made the change.
  • Access approval — new staff accounts require admin approval before accessing any data.
  • Data deletion on request — school administrators can wipe all their school's data from the admin panel at any time.

Infrastructure and subprocessors.

Cohort Logic uses the following third-party services to operate:

Provider Purpose Data stored
Supabase Database and authentication All CICO data; staff account credentials
Netlify / Cloudflare Pages Static file hosting No user data — HTML, CSS, and JS files only

What we don't do.

  • We do not sell student data or staff data to anyone, ever.
  • We do not use student data to train machine learning models.
  • We do not share data with third parties except the infrastructure providers listed above.
  • We do not serve advertising inside any Cohort Logic product.
  • We do not access your school's data except to operate and support the service.

Data retention.

Data is retained as long as your school has an active account. School administrators can delete all their school's CICO data at any time from the admin panel. Our retention schedule is:

  • Active accounts — data is retained for as long as your account is active and in use.
  • Inactive accounts — if an account goes unused for 12 months, we may notify you and then delete its data and close the account.
  • Deletion on request — a school administrator can request deletion of their school's data at any time; we complete it within 30 days.
  • Backups — deleted data may persist in encrypted backups for up to 90 days before it is overwritten.

To close an account and request full data deletion, contact us at the address below.

Your rights.

As a school administrator using Cohort Logic, you can:

  • Export or review your school's data at any time from within the app.
  • Delete individual student records or all data for your school from the admin panel.
  • Deactivate staff accounts to revoke access immediately.
  • Request a full account deletion by contacting us.

Questions.

If you have questions about this policy or want to request data deletion, reach us at hello@cohortlogic.com. We'll respond within 5 business days.
