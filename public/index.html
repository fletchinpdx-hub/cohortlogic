How Class Builder works

Four steps: import, constrain, solve, decide.

It's the same shape both our products share, and the one the rest of the suite will follow: small, careful, and built with the people who'll use it. (See how Schedule Builder works →)

01

Bring your data in

Upload your Google Sheet or CSV with students, classrooms, and the rules you need to honor. Use IDs instead of names if you'd prefer to keep things anonymized.

02

Add the human stuff

Mark students who must be separated, kids who need a specific teacher's classroom style, ELL clustering targets, IEP distribution. Every constraint your team already tracks.

03

Solve and review

Class Builder generates balanced rosters, then shows you exactly which constraints it honored and which it traded off. You make the final call.

04

Export and decide

Send the final list back to your team in Excel. Nothing is saved on our end when you're done. The decision stays yours.

What we won't compromise

The four things we hold every product to.

These come straight from the brand we're building. Every feature has to clear all four, and we'll never ship one that doesn't.

⏻ Secure

Privacy by default, not bolted on later.

We don't save or store the student data you bring in. We don't train models on your rosters. Anonymize where you can (IDs instead of names), and we'll handle the rest.

∑ Smart

A solver that shows its work.

Every placement carries an explanation: which rules were honored, which were traded off, and what the alternative would have cost.

⌖ Student focused

Decisions that survive the parent meeting.

When a parent asks why their child is in 3B, you have an answer that's actually true. Not "that's what the algorithm did."

◎ Education built

Designed with the people who run schools.

Every feature ships only after we've watched a real school leader try it. We're a small team and we'd rather build a few things well.
