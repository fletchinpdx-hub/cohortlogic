The problem

The building schedule is a thousand-piece puzzle where every piece touches every other one. Most schools still build it by hand.

Prep time, shared specials teachers, lunch waves, recess spacing, pullouts, and intervention blocks all fight for the same minutes. Move one grade's PE and three others break. It's a spreadsheet that eats weeks of summer, and one late change can quietly double-book the only music teacher for the whole year.

What we actually do Schedule Builder does the placement math (fitting every required block around your fixed times, rotating specials so no teacher is in two rooms at once) and surfaces every conflict as you go. You keep the judgement calls; it keeps the plates spinning.

Common questions

What schedulers ask first.

What kind of schools is this for? +
Schedule Builder is built for elementary and K-8 building schedules, the kind with grade-level blocks, rotating specials, lunch waves, and recess spacing. If your building runs on ELA/math blocks and a shared specials team, it's for you.
How does it handle specials? +
You define your specials (PE, music, art, library), how many times a week each class gets them, and which teachers deliver them. Schedule Builder rotates them across every class in a grade, tries to hold the same time each day, and never schedules a specials teacher in two places at once. It flags any class it can't fully cover.
Can I still adjust the schedule by hand? +
Yes. Auto-build gives you a starting point, then you can drag blocks, lock the ones you want to keep, resize them, and re-run. Your manual placements survive and the solver fills in around them. It's a tool for your judgement, not a replacement for it.
Does it connect to my SIS? +
Not today. Schedule Builder works from the school-day, block, and specials details you enter, and exports a finished schedule to Excel. There's no student data required to build a building schedule, and no SIS sync to set up.
Where is my schedule stored? +
On your device. You save your work to a file and re-open it to pick up where you left off. Your schedule isn't stored on our servers, which keeps the whole thing private by default.
