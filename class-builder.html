The problem

Class placement is still a spreadsheet problem.

Most schools build class lists with a combination of spreadsheets, sticky notes, and institutional memory. It works — until you have 400 students, 18 constraints, and a team that disagrees on what "balanced" means.

✕

No single source of truth

Multiple spreadsheet versions circulate until someone exports "final_FINAL_v3.xlsx".

✕

Constraints get lost

Keep-apart rules live in emails, sticky notes, and memory. One slip and you've got a situation in October.

✕

"Balanced" is hard to verify

It's nearly impossible to confirm equitable distribution of IEP students, scores, or demographics without writing formulas from scratch.

FAQ

Common questions.

Didn't find what you're looking for?

Ask us directly →
Does my student data go to your servers? +
No. Class Builder runs entirely in your browser. Your student data is never uploaded to or stored on Cohort Logic's servers. When you close the tab, it's gone. This is by design, not just policy.
What file formats does it accept? +
Excel files (.xlsx and .xls) via drag-and-drop or file browse. Public Google Sheets via paste-a-link. The app auto-detects column names and suggests mappings. You can adjust before proceeding.
How does the balancing algorithm work? +
Class Builder uses a snake-draft approach: students are sorted by composite score and assigned to classrooms in a 1-2-3-3-2-1 pattern to ensure even distribution. A second pass enforces separation and together constraints by swapping students between rooms. Category fields (like ethnicity) are balanced in a third pass. No black-box AI — every placement has a reason.
What if I need to make manual changes? +
The results view has drag-and-drop. You can move any student to a different class. Constraint violations are flagged the moment they occur. You can also regenerate at any time — your constraints and class configuration are preserved.
Does it work for multi-grade (split) classes? +
Yes. You can configure split classes that draw from two grade levels (e.g. a 3/4 split). Class Builder pulls approximately 50% from each grade. You set how many split classes you need and which grades they span.
Is it really free? +
Yes. Class Builder is free for any school through September 2026. No credit card required. We'll give you advance notice before any pricing changes, and we're committed to keeping it affordable for the schools that need it most. See our pricing page for more detail on our approach.
